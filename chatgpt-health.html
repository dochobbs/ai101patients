ChatGPT Health Deep Dive

How to connect your records, control your privacy, and use ChatGPT Health safely.

20 min read

What Is ChatGPT Health?

In January 2026, OpenAI launched ChatGPT Health. This feature lets you connect your medical records directly to ChatGPT. Once connected, you can ask questions about your own health data and get personalized responses.

This is a big deal. Instead of typing out your entire medical history, ChatGPT can now see your actual lab results, medications, diagnoses, and more. It can answer questions like "Are any of my medications interacting badly?" or "How has my cholesterol changed over the past year?"

The feature represents a significant shift in how patients can interact with their health data. Previously, you might have downloaded your records and manually asked AI about specific values. Now, the AI can access and analyze your comprehensive health picture—if you choose to grant it access.

How It Works: The b.well Connection

ChatGPT Health connects to your medical records through a company called b.well Connected Health. This is a health data platform that already works with thousands of healthcare systems. When you connect, b.well pulls your records and makes them available to ChatGPT through FHIR-based APIs—the modern standard for healthcare data exchange.

Understanding FHIR and Why It Matters

FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources) is the modern technical standard that allows different healthcare systems to share data. Before FHIR, getting your records from different doctors was often difficult because each system stored data differently.

b.well uses FHIR-based APIs to collect your records from various healthcare organizations in a standardized format. This is the same technology that allows Apple Health to pull in your medical records and that powers many patient portal integrations. The standardization means ChatGPT can understand and analyze your records regardless of which hospital or clinic they came from.

What Records Can ChatGPT Access?

Once connected, ChatGPT can see:

  • Lab results - Blood work, imaging reports, test results
  • Medications - Current and past prescriptions
  • Diagnoses - Conditions in your medical record
  • Procedures - Surgeries, treatments you've had
  • Immunizations - Vaccine records
  • Allergies - Documented allergies and reactions
  • Vital signs - Blood pressure, weight, height over time
  • Clinical notes - In some cases, doctors' notes from visits
Pearl

ChatGPT can only see records that are in digital form and shared through the b.well network. Older paper records, records from providers not in the network, or information your doctor hasn't entered into the system won't be visible.

Learn More: What is b.well Connected Health?

b.well Connected Health is a health technology company that specializes in aggregating health data from multiple sources into a single platform. They've built connections to thousands of healthcare organizations, pharmacies, and health plans.

The company was founded in 2015 and has focused on giving patients access to their own health data. Their platform allows consumers to pull their records from various healthcare providers and view them in one place.

For the ChatGPT Health integration, b.well acts as the intermediary that handles the technical complexity of connecting to different healthcare systems. When you authorize b.well to access your records, they use industry-standard protocols (FHIR APIs) to retrieve your data and make it available to ChatGPT.

b.well is HIPAA-compliant, meaning they follow strict federal regulations about protecting health information. However, when you authorize sharing with ChatGPT, you're making a personal choice about your data.

Step-by-Step: Connecting Your Records

Here's how to set up ChatGPT Health:

1 Open ChatGPT Settings

In the ChatGPT app or website, go to Settings > Personalization > Health. You'll see the option to "Connect health data." This feature is available to ChatGPT Plus, Team, and Enterprise subscribers.

2 Sign In to b.well

You'll be redirected to b.well to create an account or sign in. This is where you'll verify your identity and authorize access to your records. You'll need to provide personal information to confirm you are who you say you are.

3 Find Your Healthcare Providers

Search for your doctors, hospitals, and clinics. You can also connect through your health insurance plan. Select each provider you want to connect. Not all providers may be available—the network is large but not universal.

4 Authorize Access

For each provider, you'll need to authorize b.well to access your records. This usually involves logging into your patient portal or verifying your identity through other means. Some providers may require multi-factor authentication.

5 Wait for Sync

Records can take anywhere from a few minutes to 24 hours to fully sync, depending on your providers. You'll get a notification when your data is ready. Some providers update more frequently than others.

6 Review What's Connected

Once connected, review what records ChatGPT can see. Check that the information looks complete and accurate. If something seems missing, you may need to connect additional providers or check if those records are available digitally.

Try This

Before connecting, make a list of all your healthcare providers from the past few years. Having this ready will make the setup process much smoother. Include hospitals, specialists, your primary care doctor, urgent care visits, and any labs where you've had blood work done.

Learn More: Troubleshooting connection issues

If you're having trouble connecting your records, here are common issues and solutions:

Provider not found: Not all healthcare providers are in the b.well network yet. If your provider isn't listed, you may need to wait for them to join or manually upload records.

Authorization fails: Make sure you're using the correct login credentials for your patient portal. If you've never set up a patient portal account, you may need to do that first with your provider.

Incomplete records: Some providers share more data than others. Recent visits may not appear immediately. If records seem incomplete, check back in a few days.

Old records missing: Digital record-keeping has improved over time. Records from many years ago may not be available electronically.

Privacy Settings You Should Know

Connecting your health records to AI raises real privacy questions. Here's what you can control and what you should know:

What OpenAI Says About Your Data

  • Not used for training: OpenAI states health data is not used to train AI models
  • Encrypted: Data is encrypted in transit and at rest
  • You control access: You can disconnect at any time
  • HIPAA considerations: b.well is HIPAA-compliant; ChatGPT itself is not a "covered entity"
  • Data retention: Check the privacy policy for how long data is retained

Source: OpenAI Privacy Policy and ChatGPT Health documentation

Warning

Once you share health information in a conversation with ChatGPT, that conversation is stored. Even if you disconnect your health records, information you've already discussed may remain in your chat history. Be thoughtful about what you share.

Privacy Controls Available

  • Disconnect anytime: Go to Settings > Personalization > Health to disconnect
  • Delete conversations: Remove specific chats that contain health info
  • Chat history off: You can disable chat history entirely (conversations won't be saved)
  • Temporary chat: Use temporary chat mode for sensitive questions
  • Data export: You can request a copy of your data from OpenAI
  • Data deletion: You can request deletion of your account and associated data

Understanding the Privacy Tradeoffs

There's an inherent tension between AI usefulness and privacy. For ChatGPT to give you personalized health insights, it needs access to your personal health data. You need to decide if that tradeoff makes sense for you.

Consider: Are you comfortable with a technology company having access to your complete medical history? What's the value you're getting in return? Are there alternatives (like manually sharing specific information) that would work for your needs?

Learn More: Understanding HIPAA and AI

HIPAA (the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) protects your health information when it's held by healthcare providers and insurance companies. However, when you voluntarily share your data with a consumer app like ChatGPT, different rules apply.

b.well is HIPAA-compliant, meaning they follow strict rules about protecting your data when they retrieve it from healthcare providers. But once you authorize sharing with ChatGPT, you're the one making the choice to share.

This isn't necessarily bad, but it means you should be informed about what you're agreeing to. Read the privacy policies and understand your rights.

Key things to understand:

  • HIPAA applies to "covered entities" like doctors and insurers, not all tech companies
  • When you authorize sharing, you're exercising your patient rights to your own data
  • Consumer apps are governed by their privacy policies and terms of service
  • The FTC can take action against companies that violate their privacy promises
Learn More: Questions to ask yourself before connecting

Before connecting your health records, consider these questions:

What do I hope to gain? Understanding your goals helps you decide if the benefits outweigh the privacy considerations.

Am I comfortable with this company having my health data? Research the company's track record and privacy practices.

What's the worst that could happen? Consider potential risks like data breaches, policy changes, or unintended uses of your data.

Are there alternatives? Could you get similar benefits by manually sharing specific records or using privacy-preserving approaches?

Can I easily disconnect? Make sure you understand how to remove your data if you change your mind.

Beyond Medical Records: Other Health Data

ChatGPT Health can also connect to wellness apps and devices, giving it access to data beyond your official medical records:

Supported Connections

  • Apple Health: Steps, heart rate, sleep, workouts, and more from your iPhone or Apple Watch
  • Peloton: Workout history, heart rate during exercise, activity trends
  • MyFitnessPal: Nutrition tracking, calorie intake, meal logging
  • Oura Ring: Sleep data, readiness scores, activity tracking
  • Whoop: Strain, recovery, and sleep metrics
  • Other fitness apps: The list of supported apps continues to grow

Combining Medical and Wellness Data

One powerful use case is combining your official medical records with wellness data. For example, you could ask: "Based on my recent blood pressure readings at the doctor and my activity and sleep data from my watch, what patterns do you notice?"

This kind of holistic view wasn't easily possible before. Your doctor sees your clinical visits; your fitness app sees your daily activity. Now AI can help connect these data sources and identify patterns across them.

Pearl

Connecting wellness data can be useful for questions like "Based on my recent sleep patterns and activity, what might explain why I'm feeling tired?" But remember, the same privacy considerations apply. More connected data means more information about you in one place.

Try This

If you use Apple Health, try connecting it to ChatGPT and asking: "Looking at my sleep and activity data from the past month, do you notice any patterns?" This can be a good way to spot trends you might have missed. Then bring interesting findings to your doctor.

What to Ask ChatGPT Health

Once your records are connected, here are some useful ways to use it:

Understanding Your Data

  • "Explain my most recent blood test results in simple terms."
  • "What does my A1C level mean, and is mine in a healthy range?"
  • "Summarize my health visits from the past year."
  • "What conditions do I have documented in my records?"
  • "Are there any lab values that have been flagged as abnormal?"

Tracking Changes

  • "How has my cholesterol changed over the past three years?"
  • "Show me the trend in my blood pressure readings."
  • "Are there any lab values that have been consistently outside normal range?"
  • "Has my weight trended up or down over the past year?"
  • "Compare my kidney function tests from this year to last year."

Preparing for Appointments

  • "Based on my records, what questions should I ask my doctor at my next visit?"
  • "Create a summary of my current medications and conditions for a new doctor."
  • "What preventive screenings might I be due for based on my age and history?"
  • "Help me prepare a one-page health summary for my upcoming specialist visit."
  • "Based on my health history, what lifestyle factors should I discuss with my doctor?"

Understanding Medications

  • "Are any of my current medications known to interact with each other?"
  • "Explain what each of my medications is for."
  • "What are the common side effects of my medications?"
  • "Based on my records, are there any medications I might be taking duplicates of?"
  • "I'm experiencing [symptom]. Could any of my medications be causing this?"

Connecting Patterns

  • "Looking at my records and my fitness data, do you see any correlations?"
  • "My energy has been low lately. Based on my recent labs and sleep data, what might explain this?"
  • "Are there any patterns in when my symptoms seem to flare up based on my health data?"
Warning

Never change your medications based on ChatGPT's responses. If you have concerns about drug interactions or side effects, bring them up with your doctor or pharmacist. AI can help you identify questions to ask, not give you medical orders.

Learn More: Sample conversation with ChatGPT Health

Here's an example of how a productive conversation with ChatGPT Health might go:

You: "Based on my records, help me understand my recent lab results. Focus on anything that's outside normal range or has changed significantly."

ChatGPT: [Analyzes your connected lab results and provides a summary of notable values, explaining what they mean in simple terms]

You: "My triglycerides seem high. What does that mean and what questions should I ask my doctor about it?"

ChatGPT: [Explains triglycerides, what elevated levels might indicate, and suggests specific questions for your doctor]

You: "Looking at my records over time, has this been a consistent issue?"

ChatGPT: [Shows the trend in your triglyceride levels over available records and notes any patterns]

Notice how this conversation uses AI to understand and prepare, not to diagnose or decide on treatment.

What ChatGPT Health Cannot Do

Even with your records connected, ChatGPT has important limitations:

  • Cannot diagnose: It can discuss what your data might suggest, but cannot tell you what condition you have
  • Cannot prescribe: It cannot tell you what medications to take or change your treatment
  • Cannot see everything: Records not in the b.well network won't appear
  • Cannot know context: It doesn't know what you were feeling when tests were done or the full picture your doctor sees
  • Can make mistakes: It might misinterpret data or give incorrect information
  • Cannot replace clinical judgment: Doctors consider many factors AI doesn't have access to
  • Cannot provide emergency care: In a medical emergency, call 911, not AI

Understanding AI Limitations with Health Data

Even when AI has access to your comprehensive health records, it's working with incomplete information. Your health is more than data points in a database.

Your doctor considers things like: How do you look today? What's your affect? What did you tell them about your stress levels? What do they know about your family situation? What's their clinical intuition based on years of experience?

AI can analyze the data in your records, but it can't replace the holistic assessment that comes from a real clinical relationship.

Learn More: Why AI can't replace your doctor's interpretation

When your doctor looks at your lab results, they see them in context. They know you were dehydrated that day. They remember you mentioned stress at work. They can examine you and notice things that aren't in any database.

AI sees numbers and text. It can pattern-match against what it's learned, but it lacks the human judgment that comes from actually knowing you as a person.

This is why ChatGPT Health is best used as a research assistant that helps you understand and prepare, not as a replacement for the clinical relationship with your healthcare team.

Consider: A slightly elevated lab value might be concerning in one patient and completely expected in another based on their history. AI sees the number; your doctor sees the context.

Learn More: When AI interpretation might be misleading

There are specific situations where AI interpretation of your health data could lead you astray:

Reference ranges vary: What's "normal" can differ by age, sex, and other factors. AI might flag something as abnormal that's actually fine for you.

Context matters: A high heart rate during exercise is normal; the same heart rate at rest might be concerning. AI may not always have this context.

Correlations aren't causation: AI might notice two things happening together without understanding which caused which, or if they're even related.

Rare conditions: AI has less training data on uncommon conditions and may be less accurate when dealing with them.

Individual variation: Your normal might be different from population averages. A value that's fine for you might look concerning to AI.
