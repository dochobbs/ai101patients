When to Trust AI vs. Call Your Doctor

Red flags that need human care. When AI helps, when it doesn't, and when to pick up the phone.

10 min read

Never Use AI for Emergencies

Call 911 or go to the ER immediately if you have:

  • Chest pain or pressure
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Sudden severe headache
  • Signs of stroke (face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty)
  • Severe allergic reaction
  • Uncontrolled bleeding
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Thoughts of harming yourself or others

AI cannot assess emergencies. Don't waste time typingâ€”call for help.

Trust Your Gut

If something feels wrong, it probably deserves attention from a real person. AI can't see you. It can't hear the worry in your voice. It can't notice that you look pale or are breathing fast.

Your instincts matter. If you're anxious enough to be searching symptoms at 2 AM, that anxiety itself is information. Don't let AI reassure you out of seeking care you might need.

Pearl

"I'd rather look silly for going in and it's nothing, than stay home and it's something." This is a good philosophy. AI can't make this judgment call for you.

When AI and Your Doctor Disagree

What if AI tells you something different from what your doctor said? Trust your doctor.

Here's why:

  • Your doctor has examined you. AI hasn't.
  • Your doctor knows your full history. AI knows what you've told it.
  • Your doctor can order tests and adjust treatment. AI can only talk.
  • Your doctor is accountable for your care. AI is not.

That said, if AI raises a question that concerns you, bring it up with your doctor. "I read that some people with my condition also need X. Is that something I should ask about?" A good doctor will welcome your questions.

Warning

Never stop taking a medication or change your treatment because AI suggested it. Always talk to your prescribing provider first. AI doesn't know why your doctor made the choices they made for your specific situation.

Using AI to Support (Not Replace) Care

The best use of AI is to make your conversations with your healthcare team better:

  • Before appointments: Use AI to generate questions, understand your condition, organize your concerns
  • After appointments: Use AI to clarify things you didn't understand, research terms the doctor used
  • Between appointments: Use AI for general learning about your health, not for diagnosing new problems
Try This

After your next doctor's appointment, try asking ChatGPT: "My doctor said I have [condition]. Can you explain what that means in simple terms?" Then bring any follow-up questions to your next visit.

Learn More: The "second opinion" trap

Some people use AI as a "second opinion" on their doctor's advice. This can be problematic for several reasons:

AI doesn't have the context your doctor has. It doesn't know why certain choices were made for you specifically. It might suggest an alternative that sounds reasonable in general but isn't right for your situation.

If you genuinely want a second opinion, get one from another doctor who can actually examine you and review your full recordsâ€”not from a chatbot.
