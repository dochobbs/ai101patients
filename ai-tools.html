AI Tools You Should Know

The chatbots, search engines, and specialized tools people are using for health questions.

15 min read

The AI Landscape

There are now dozens of AI tools available, and it can be overwhelming to know which to use. This guide focuses on the ones people actually use for health questions—and what each is good (and not good) at.

Pearl

No single AI tool is "best" for everything. Different tools have different strengths. Knowing what each does well helps you pick the right one for your question.

The Big Chatbots

These are the main AI assistants people use for general conversations, including health questions.

ChatGPT (OpenAI)

Free + Paid tiers Web, iOS, Android Most popular

The most widely used AI chatbot. Now includes ChatGPT Health, which can connect to your medical records. Good for general health questions, explaining concepts, and preparing for appointments.

Best for: General health questions, understanding medical terms, conversation-style learning

Limitation: Can hallucinate, especially with specific medical facts

Claude (Anthropic)

Free + Paid tiers Web, iOS, Android

Known for thoughtful, nuanced responses. Often better at acknowledging uncertainty and limitations. Good for longer conversations where you want careful reasoning.

Best for: Complex questions requiring nuance, understanding different perspectives

Limitation: No health records integration (as of early 2026)

Gemini (Google)

Free + Paid tiers Web, iOS, Android

Google's AI, integrated with Google services. Can search the web for current information. Good for questions where recent information matters.

Best for: Questions needing current information, integration with Google ecosystem

Limitation: Sometimes pulls unreliable web sources

Copilot (Microsoft)

Free + Paid tiers Web, Windows, Edge

Microsoft's AI assistant, integrated with Bing search and Microsoft products. Good for users already in the Microsoft ecosystem.

Best for: Windows users, integration with Microsoft 365

Limitation: Less commonly used for health questions specifically

AI Search Tools

These tools combine AI with web search, giving you answers with sources.

Perplexity

Free + Paid tiers Web, iOS, Android

AI-powered search that provides answers with cited sources. You can see where the information came from and verify it yourself. Increasingly popular for health research.

Best for: Research questions where you want to verify sources

Limitation: Quality depends on source websites it pulls from

Google AI Overviews

Free Google Search

AI-generated summaries that appear at the top of Google search results. You're probably already seeing these. They synthesize information from multiple web sources.

Best for: Quick answers while doing regular web searches

Limitation: Sometimes summarizes unreliable sources; has had notable errors

In the News

2025-2026: AI search has become mainstream. Most people now encounter AI-generated health information whether they're specifically seeking it or not, just by using regular search engines.

Specialized Tools

These tools have specific purposes beyond general conversation.

NotebookLM (Google)

Free Web

Upload documents (like your discharge summary or a research article) and ask questions about them. The AI only answers based on what you uploaded—reducing hallucination risk.

Best for: Understanding medical documents you've received, summarizing long articles

Limitation: Only knows what you upload to it

Sora 2 (OpenAI)

Paid Web

AI video generation. Less directly relevant to health questions, but increasingly used for creating educational content and explanations.

Best for: Visual learners who want to see concepts explained

Limitation: Generated content may not be medically accurate

Nano Banana Pro (Google)

Free + Paid Web

Google's image generation tool. Can create visual explanations of concepts. Use with caution for medical imagery—AI-generated medical images can be misleading.

Best for: Creating simple visual aids or diagrams

Limitation: Don't use for actual medical imagery or diagnosis

Try This

If you have a medical document you're trying to understand (like discharge instructions), try uploading it to NotebookLM and asking: "What are the most important things I need to do based on this document?"

Health-Focused AI Tools

These tools are specifically designed for health questions.

Ada Health

Free iOS, Android

AI-powered symptom checker that asks questions about your symptoms and suggests possible conditions. Developed with medical expertise.

Best for: Initial symptom assessment before deciding to call your doctor

Limitation: Cannot diagnose—only suggests possibilities to discuss with your doctor

K Health

Free + Paid iOS, Android

AI symptom checker that can also connect you with real doctors for telehealth visits. The AI helps gather your information before a human consultation.

Best for: Getting from symptoms to doctor consultation efficiently

Limitation: Full features require payment; not a replacement for your regular doctor

Learn More: Should I use health-specific AI vs. general chatbots?

Health-specific tools like Ada are designed with medical input and often have better safety guardrails. They're usually better for symptom checking.

General chatbots like ChatGPT are more flexible—you can ask anything—but have less medical-specific safety features. They're often better for general learning and preparing questions.

For symptom questions: consider health-specific tools first. For understanding and learning: general chatbots work well. For anything serious: talk to a real healthcare provider.

Key Takeaways

  • Different AI tools have different strengths—match the tool to your question
  • ChatGPT is most popular and now offers health records integration
  • Perplexity and similar tools let you verify sources
  • NotebookLM is great for understanding documents you upload
  • Health-specific apps like Ada are designed for symptom checking
  • No AI tool replaces your doctor—they're research assistants, not providers
