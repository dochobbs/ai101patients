Preparing for Doctor Appointments

Use AI to organize your thoughts, prepare questions, and make the most of limited time with your doctor.

12 min read

Why Preparation Matters

Doctor appointments are short. The average primary care visit is about 15-20 minutes, and that includes time for the doctor to review your chart, examine you, and document the visit. Your actual conversation time might be 8-10 minutes.

If you walk in unprepared, it's easy to forget important symptoms, ramble about things that aren't priorities, or leave without asking your most important questions. AI can help you prepare so you make the most of every minute.

Pearl

Research shows that patients who prepare questions before appointments report higher satisfaction and better understanding of their care plan. AI makes this preparation faster and more thorough.

Step 1: Organize Your Symptoms

Before your appointment, use AI to help you create a clear, organized summary of what's been going on. This saves time and helps your doctor understand your situation quickly.

Prompt to try:

I have an appointment with my doctor about [main concern]. Help me organize the following symptoms into a clear summary: [List your symptoms, when they started, how often they happen, what makes them better or worse, etc.] Please format this as a brief summary I can hand to my doctor or read quickly at the start of my appointment.

What to Include

  • Timeline: When did this start? Has it changed over time?
  • Frequency: How often does it happen? Constant? Occasional?
  • Severity: On a 1-10 scale, how bad is it at worst? On average?
  • Triggers: Does anything make it worse? Better?
  • Impact: How is this affecting your daily life?
  • What you've tried: Any treatments, medications, or home remedies?
Try This

Before your next appointment, spend 5 minutes describing your symptoms to AI in your own words. Then ask it to organize what you said into a clear, one-paragraph summary. Bring that summary to your appointment.

Step 2: Prepare Your Questions

You always have questions for your doctor. But under the pressure of a short appointment, it's easy to forget them or not know how to phrase them. AI can help you prepare a prioritized list.

Prompt to try:

I'm seeing my [type of doctor] about [reason for visit]. My background: [brief relevant history - age, conditions, medications] Help me create a list of 5-7 questions I should consider asking. Focus on: - Understanding my condition or symptoms - Treatment options and what to expect - Warning signs I should watch for - Lifestyle changes that might help Put the most important questions first.

Types of Questions Worth Asking

  • Understanding: "Can you explain what this means in simple terms?"
  • Options: "What are my treatment options, and what do you recommend?"
  • Next steps: "What should I do next? When should I follow up?"
  • Warning signs: "What symptoms should make me call you or go to the ER?"
  • Lifestyle: "Are there lifestyle changes that would help with this?"
  • Medications: "What side effects should I watch for? How long should I take this?"
Pearl

Bring your question list on paper or your phone. If you run out of time, ask your doctor: "I have a few more questions - should I schedule another appointment, or can I send them through the patient portal?"

Step 3: Understand Before You Go

If you're seeing a specialist or discussing a specific condition, AI can help you understand the basics before your appointment. This lets you have a more informed conversation.

Prompt to try:

I'm seeing a [specialist type] for the first time because [reason]. Please give me a brief overview of: 1. What this type of doctor specializes in 2. What I should expect during the visit 3. Basic terms related to [condition] I might hear 4. What information or records I should bring Keep it simple and focused on what will help me prepare.
In the News

Research Finding: Studies show that patients who understand their conditions better are more likely to follow treatment plans and have better health outcomes. Preparation isn't just about efficiency - it leads to better care.

What to Bring vs. What to Verify

AI can help you prepare materials to bring to your appointment. But some things AI produces should be verified rather than handed directly to your doctor.

Good to Bring

  • Your symptom summary (organized by AI)
  • Your question list (generated with AI help)
  • Timeline of when symptoms started
  • List of current medications and supplements
  • Notes on what you've already tried
  • Your goals for the visit

Verify First

  • Any "diagnosis" AI suggested - bring as a question
  • Statistics or research AI cited - may be made up
  • Treatment recommendations - let your doctor decide
  • Drug interaction warnings - pharmacist should verify
  • Claims that contradict previous medical advice
Warning

Don't tell your doctor "AI diagnosed me with X." Instead say: "I was reading about my symptoms and X came up. Is that something we should consider?" This respects your doctor's expertise while still raising the topic.

During and After the Appointment

During Your Appointment

  • Lead with your main concern: "The most important thing I want to discuss today is..."
  • Use your prepared summary to explain symptoms clearly and efficiently
  • Take notes, or ask if you can record the conversation
  • Ask for clarification: "Can you explain that in simpler terms?"
  • Before you leave, confirm: "So the next steps are..." to make sure you understand

After Your Appointment

AI can help you make sense of what happened during your appointment:

Prompt to try:

I just had a doctor appointment about [reason]. Here's what my doctor told me: [Summarize what you remember - diagnosis, treatment plan, follow-up instructions] Can you help me: 1. Understand any medical terms they used 2. Organize the next steps I need to take 3. Identify anything I should ask about if I'm unclear
Try This

After your next appointment, write down everything you remember within an hour. Then ask AI to help you organize it. If there are gaps or confusion, use your patient portal to send a follow-up question to your doctor's office.

Learn More: Using AI with your patient portal messages

Many patients now use their patient portal to send messages to their healthcare team. AI can help you write clearer, more effective messages.

Try: "Help me write a clear, concise message to my doctor about [concern]. Include relevant background and specific questions."

This helps you communicate effectively without writing an essay or forgetting important details. Keep messages focused - your doctor reads many messages a day.

Special Situations

Preparing for a Second Opinion

Prompt to try:

I'm getting a second opinion about [condition/diagnosis/recommended treatment]. My current situation: [brief background] My current doctor recommends: [current recommendation] My concerns are: [your concerns] Help me prepare questions to ask the new doctor. I want to understand if there are alternative approaches without undermining my current doctor.

Advocating for Yourself or a Family Member

Prompt to try:

I'm accompanying [relationship] to a doctor appointment about [concern]. They have [relevant history]. I'm worried the doctor might dismiss their concerns because [reason - e.g., previous experience, communication challenges]. Help me prepare: 1. How to clearly present their symptoms 2. Questions to ensure thorough evaluation 3. Phrases to advocate for them respectfully but firmly

Key Takeaways

  • Use AI to organize your symptoms into a clear, concise summary before appointments
  • Prepare a prioritized list of questions - you may only have time for a few
  • Research your condition basics so you can have an informed conversation
  • Bring AI-organized materials, but verify AI-generated claims before presenting them as fact
  • After appointments, use AI to help understand and organize what you learned
  • Lead with your main concern - don't save the most important thing for last
