Something Big Just Happened

In January 2026, OpenAI launched ChatGPT Health—a new feature that lets you connect your medical records directly to ChatGPT. With a few clicks, the AI can now see your lab results, medications, diagnoses, and health history.

This isn't science fiction. It's available now. And over 40 million people already ask ChatGPT health questions every single day.

In the News

January 2026: OpenAI launches ChatGPT Health, allowing users to connect medical records from over 2.2 million healthcare providers. The feature works with Apple Health, Peloton, MyFitnessPal, and other wellness apps.

This is powerful. And a little scary. AI can help you understand your health in ways that weren't possible before. But it can also make mistakes, misunderstand your situation, or give you advice that sounds confident but is completely wrong.

That's why this guide exists.

What You'll Learn

This guide teaches you how to use AI tools for your health—safely and effectively. No technical background needed. No medical degree required.

We cover three main areas:

1. Safety First

Before you dive into using AI with your health information, you need to understand the risks. What can go wrong? How do you protect your privacy? When should you absolutely NOT trust what AI tells you?

2. Practical Skills

Once you know the guardrails, you'll learn how to actually use these tools. How to ask better questions. How to evaluate AI responses. How to prepare for doctor appointments using AI as a helper.

3. Going Deeper (Optional)

For the curious: how does AI actually work? Why does it make the mistakes it makes? What's coming next in healthcare AI?

Pearl

AI is a tool, not a doctor. The goal of this guide isn't to replace your healthcare team—it's to help you become a better-informed patient who uses AI as one resource among many.

How to Use This Guide

Each module takes about 10-15 minutes to read. You can work through them in order, or jump to what's most relevant to you.

Our recommendation: Start with the "Safety First" modules before diving into practical skills. Understanding the risks upfront will help you use AI more confidently.

Who This Guide Is For

This guide is for you if:

  • You've used or want to use AI tools like ChatGPT for health questions
  • You're managing a chronic condition and want AI to help you stay informed
  • You're a caregiver helping a family member navigate healthcare
  • You're curious about ChatGPT Health and want to know if it's safe
  • You want to be a more informed, empowered patient

What This Guide Is NOT

This is not medical advice. AI tools are not a replacement for your doctor, nurse practitioner, or other healthcare providers. This guide helps you use AI as one tool among many—while knowing its limits and when to seek real medical care.
